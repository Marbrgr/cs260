Cryptocurrency

WHAT IS A "CRYPTOCURRENCY"

A cryptocurrency (often called just a "crypto") is a digital or a virtual currency that is secured by cryptography, which makes it difficult or impossible to conterfeit or double-spend. Many cryptocurrencies are decentralized networks that are based on blockchain technology. Cryptocurrencies often attract users because they are usually decentralized, meaning they are not controlled by one central authority. We will go over a few of the most notable cryptos, where they came from, what they're valued at, and why they're important.

BITCOIN

Bitcoin was invented and implemented by the person or persons who are pseudonymously as Satoshi Nakamoto. Nakamoto published a paper on October 31, 2008 which was titled "Bitcoin: A Peer-to-Peer Electronic Cash System." The first bitcoin transaction on the blockchain was executed and the first block was mined on January 3, 2009. Initially bitcoin had no monetary value, but the idea has gained traction and the value of bitcoin has skyrocketed to currenlty be a whopping $38,549 US dollars. Bitcoin's creator Satoshi Nakamoto continues to remain unknown to this day.

SHIBA INU

The Shiba Inu token is another cryptocurrency that was created by an anonymous group known only as "Ryoshi". The coin was started in August of 2020 and is known by many as a "meme coin", a cryptocurrency that originated from an internet meme (see Dogecoin). It is named after the Shiba Inu, a Japanese breed of dog. In an interesting marketing attempt, the creators of Shiba Inu sent 50% of the Shiba coin to Vitalik Buterin. Many believe that this was an attempt to increase trust in the currency, as the founder of a notable currency now held billions of Shiba Inu tokens. Many are speculative of the success of the Shiba token in the future, but other meme coins have had large increases in value in the past.
