Blockchain Basics

What is it?

A blockchain is exactly what it sounds like: a digital CHAIN of BLOCKS. Each of the blocks in the chain stores some kind of information. In the case of Bitcoin, the blocks in the chain contain parts of a ledger that contains Bitcoin transactions. There isn't just one blockchain. The blockchain is a type of structure, just like an array or a tree (if you've had any programming experience).

Where did it come from?

Though blockchain has only recently become a well-known, the concept for a blockchain-like system was proposed by a cryptographer named David Chaum in 1982. Chaum's proposal was further researched years later in 1991 by Stuart Haber and W. Scott Stornetta. Though it had been proposed decades earlier, blockchain only came to light in 2008, when an unknown person (or persons) called Satoshi Nakamoto published a paper that outlined a decentralized currency called "Bitcoin".
